Hi, I'm Janelle Wellons

I am an instrument operations engineer working at the NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory in Pasadena, CA and I am passionate about STEM outreach! I never could have imagined that I would one day be commanding scientific instruments in space, but here I am. I grew up in New Jersey and graduated from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) with a Bachelor's degree in Aerospace Engineering. My first job at JPL was on the Cassini mission at Saturn, taking images using the Imaging Science Subsystem and Visible and Infrared Mapping Spectrometer instruments. Now I work on a lunar and Earth mission. Learn more about my journey in an article posted on the NASA JPL site. You can also learn about my personal journey with mental illness in a short memoir I penned called When the World Was Upside Down. E-book profits subsidize my outreach efforts so I thank you in advance for your support!

Inspiring Future Rocket Scientists

I am passionate about STEM outreach for underserved youth. Whether I'm teaching space camp in South Korea or educating a class about deep space operations -- I love showing students that they are capable of learning about space! I am currently on the board of directors for the Pasadena YWCA, a non-profit organization dedicated to eliminating racism and empowering women. I also am on the board for Magnitude.io, a company that aims to provide tools to educate the next generation in STEM. I am constantly on the lookout for avenues to spread my message and encourage young people to shoot for the stars!

Speaking Engagements and Requests

I am happy to speak to a wide variety of audiences in a range of formats. Whether it is a small classroom presentation, a podcast, panel discussion, mentorship program or virtual talk for college students, I can be maleable to the need. Please reach out to me with any inquiries.

Achieving Dreams, Reaching the Stars

This talk centers around my personal story of successes and failures. I cover how I came to learn about and attend MIT as well as how I eventually got the job at NASA JPL. I also provide some brief background on what I currently work on and generally aim to inspire the audience and leave them with a desire to pursue their dreams, despite doubt and naysayers, whatever they may be.

Connecting Virtually

I am always finding ways to keep audiences engaged during these difficult times. I am able to give the same experience of presentations virtually, with the added bonus of being able to take audience questions in real-time via chat features.

Inspiring the Next Generation

Finding ways to connect with young people, especially from disadvantaged backgrounds, is one of my goals. I often chat with students one-on-one, participate in mentorship programs, and give my advice for navigating school, choosing a major, landing internships, interviews, resume creation, and any other questions a student may have.
